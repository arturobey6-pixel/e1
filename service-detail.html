Years of Gloss, One Application Ceramic Coating Done Right!

Ceramic coating is our flagship service — and the smartest investment you can make in your car's paint. We bond a professional-grade 9H ceramic layer to your clear coat, creating a hydrophobic shield that repels water, road salt, UV rays, and city grime for years, not weeks. Because a coating is only as good as the surface beneath it, every application includes full decontamination and machine polishing first, so the paint we lock in is flawless. The result: a deeper-than-showroom gloss, effortless washes, and protection that truly lasts.

Step 1
Deep Cleaning & Decontamination

We hand wash the vehicle, then clay bar and chemically decontaminate the paint to remove every trace of iron, tar, and fallout.

Step 2
Paint Correction & Swirl Removal

Machine polishing removes swirls, light scratches, and oxidation, because the coating permanently locks in whatever lies beneath it.

Step 3
Ceramic Coating Application

We apply the 9H ceramic coating panel by panel in a dust-controlled bay, leveling each section by hand for a uniform finish.

Step 4
Curing, Inspection & Care Guide

The coating cures under infrared lamps, then passes a final light inspection. You leave with simple aftercare instructions.

FAQs : Everything About Ceramic Coating

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that chemically bonds to your car's clear coat, forming a hard, glossy, hydrophobic layer of protection that far outlasts wax or sealant.

With basic care, our professional coatings protect for 2–5 years depending on the package you choose — even through NYC winters and daily street parking.

Plan on 1–2 days. Decontamination and paint correction take most of that time, and the coating itself needs several hours to cure before pickup.

It resists light swirls, chemical etching, and UV fading, but it is not armor — for impact protection, ask us about pairing it with paint protection film.

Avoid washing for the first week while the coating fully cures. After that, a gentle hand wash every few weeks is all it takes to keep the gloss alive.
